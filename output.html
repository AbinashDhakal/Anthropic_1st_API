# One-Day Meal Plan for Marathon Training

Here is a one-day meal plan for an athlete aiming to carbohydrate load and optimize hydration for high-endurance marathon training:

* **Calorie Target:** Approximately 3200 calories
* **Macronutrient Breakdown:** Protein (120g), Fat (85g), Carbs (480g)

## Meal Plan:

* **Breakfast (7:00 AM):** Oatmeal (100g dry weight) with banana (150g), honey (30g), and whole milk (240g). Side of whole wheat toast (60g) with almond butter (20g).
    * Protein: 18g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Mid-Morning Snack (10:00 AM):** Sports drink (500ml) with glucose and electrolytes. Energy bar (65g) with dates and oats.
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 6g, Carbs: 75g

* **Lunch (1:00 PM):** Pasta (120g dry weight) with lean ground turkey (120g) and tomato-based sauce (150g). Mixed green salad (150g) with olive oil dressing (15g). Orange (150g).
    * Protein: 35g, Fat: 15g, Carbs: 85g

* **Afternoon Snack (3:30 PM):** White rice cakes (50g) with honey (20g) and peanut butter (25g). Apple (120g).
    * Protein: 9g, Fat: 12g, Carbs: 95g

* **Pre-Training Snack (5:00 PM):** Bagel (70g) with jam (30g) and banana (100g).
    * Protein: 8g, Fat: 3g, Carbs: 80g

* **Dinner (7:30 PM):** Grilled chicken breast (150g) with sweet potato (200g, cooked) and steamed green beans (150g). White rice (80g dry weight).
    * Protein: 38g, Fat: 18g, Carbs: 75g

* **Evening Snack (9:00 PM):** Greek yogurt (200g) with granola (40g) and berries (80g).
    * Protein: 16g, Fat: 19g, Carbs: 55g

This meal plan emphasizes high-quality carbohydrates for glycogen storage, adequate protein for muscle recovery, strategic timing around training sessions, and consistent hydration support for marathon preparation.
